The Four Pillars

Saju (四柱) literally means "Four Pillars." Each pillar corresponds to a moment of your birth — the year, the month, the day, and the hour. Each pillar is composed of two characters (one Heavenly Stem and one Earthly Branch), making your full chart eight characters total — which is why Korean Saju is also called Pal-ja (八字), meaning "the eight characters."

Saju vs Western Astrology

Western astrology divides humanity into 12 sun signs. Saju recognizes 518,400 unique combinations (60 Galja × 60 × 60 × 24). It is the difference between calling someone "Capricorn" and calling someone "Yang Earth Tiger born in Yin Wood Rabbit year, on the third hour after sunset."

Saju doesn't predict; it reveals patterns. Your strengths, your blind spots, your luck cycles (大運), and the years that will challenge or reward you most — all written in the eight characters of your birth.

Can Foreigners Get a Saju Reading?

Yes. Saju is the language of the universe at the moment of your birth — it doesn't require Korean ancestry. Whether you were born in São Paulo, Manila, or Toronto, your four pillars exist. Pillar Seer handles the timezone and longitude correction automatically. Your stars are already aligned.
