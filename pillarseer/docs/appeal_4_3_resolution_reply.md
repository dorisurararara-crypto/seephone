Dear App Review Team,

Thank you for the detailed feedback on Guideline 4.3(a). We respectfully disagree with the classification of Pillarseer as a generic astrology / fortune-telling app, and we would like to explain the original work that distinguishes this submission from existing apps in the category.

**1. The app is built around K-pop celebrity content, not generic horoscope output.**

Pillarseer ships with an original database of **203 K-pop idols** (IU, V of BTS, Jennie of BLACKPINK, Karina of aespa, and 199 others), each with a publicly known birth date sourced from Korean Wikipedia and mapped to Korean traditional day-pillar charts. We also include **31 long-form celebrity readings** and a "최애의 사주 (My Favorite's Chart)" feature where fans browse, compare, and learn about their favorite idols. This is entertainment content for K-pop fandom — a use case that no horoscope or zodiac app on the App Store currently serves.

We have updated the binary so that immediately after a user enters their birth date, the app opens onto this K-pop celebrity tab, making the differentiated content the first surface a reviewer sees.

**2. The app contains an original literary work — 66 episodes of a Korean web-novel-style "past-life" series.**

This is narrative fiction tied to the user's day pillar, written by hand in Korean (66 episodes) and English (66 episodes), in the format of Korean web fiction (인터넷소설). It is not generated horoscope text. This alone is a substantial body of original written content that is not present in any other app in the category.

**3. The app ships a "Music Pharmacy" feature — personalized song prescriptions.**

Users receive curated K-pop and OST song recommendations matched to their personality profile. This is a music discovery feature, not a horoscope.

**4. The app contains over 1,400 hand-written character paragraphs across 17 categories.**

These are authored Korean essays about personality, relationships, work, money, and health — written and edited by our team, not algorithmically generated horoscope strings. Full English translations are also included, including 210 English celebrity body texts.

**5. The framework is Korean cultural heritage (사주, Saju / Four Pillars of Destiny), not Western astrology.**

Saju is a 1,000-year-old Korean cultural personality system, structurally and historically distinct from Western astrology or zodiac reports. Pillarseer presents this as a cultural and self-reflection framework with educational explanations of 십신, 오행, 격국, and 용신 — Korean concepts that have no counterpart in the apps the guideline targets.

**6. Monetization is a one-time premium pack (non-subscription).**

A single non-consumable IAP unlocks the K-pop compatibility tool, the 66-episode past-life series, the music pharmacy, and the full 17-category readings. We did not adopt the recurring-subscription pattern common to the category.

We have also updated the App Store listing copy and screenshots to lead with the K-pop celebrity content and the original 66-episode series, so that the differentiation is immediately visible in the product page.

We respectfully ask the review team to look specifically at the "최애의 사주" tab (now the first tab after onboarding), the past-life episode list, and the music pharmacy screen. We believe these are clearly distinct from the apps the guideline aims to filter, and we would be grateful for a second review with this context in mind.

Thank you for your time and for the opportunity to clarify.
